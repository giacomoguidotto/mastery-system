Lesson 0000

Why RAG Evaluation Matters

Before metrics and dataset rows, get the map: what RAG systems let you build, what can go wrong, and why evaluating them is an engineering advantage.

The plain idea

A RAG system answers by retrieving relevant external knowledge, putting that knowledge into the model's context, and asking the model to answer from it. The model still generates the answer, but the facts come from a source you control.

Primary sources: the original RAG paper by Lewis et al. for the parametric plus non-parametric memory idea, and the LangChain RAG tutorial for the practical Q&A-over-documents system shape.

What you get from knowing RAG

Private knowledge

You can build assistants over product docs, codebases, support notes, policies, research notes, or this PMP repo without training a new model.

Updateable answers

You can change the source documents and rerun the system. That is cheaper and more inspectable than trying to bake every fact into model weights.

Source-grounded output

You can ask the system to show where an answer came from, which makes review, debugging, and trust much more practical.

Backend leverage

You learn the moving parts behind many AI product systems: ingestion, chunking, embeddings, indexes, retrieval, prompting, generation, and evals.

This module starts at evaluation because it forces you to see the whole system. A retrieval miss means the answer never had the right evidence. A generation drift means the evidence was present but the model mishandled it. A missing-source failure means the corpus cannot answer the question yet.

Why evaluation is the lever

Without evaluation, RAG demos are easy to fool yourself with: one good answer can hide a weak retriever, a lucky model prior, or a vague dataset. With evaluation, each bad answer becomes a next experiment.

Bad answer
  -> Did the source contain the fact?
  -> Did retrieval find the fact?
  -> Did generation use the fact?
  -> Was the expected answer clear?

The point is not to worship metrics. The point is to build a loop that tells you what to fix.
